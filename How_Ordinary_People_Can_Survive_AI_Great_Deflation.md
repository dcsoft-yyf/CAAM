## I. Your Colleague Didn't Disappear—He Was Just Distilled into Tokens

Your colleague in the next cubicle, ["Zhang San, the Obedient Disciple of the Law"], was still discussing lunch plans with you last Friday. This Monday, his cubicle is empty, his computer confiscated, his access card deactivated. HR calls this "normal personnel optimization."

But something feels wrong.

The next day, you open the company's new AI assistant and discover it answers questions with his mannerisms, his phrasing habits, even his occasional sense of humor. You try inputting his favorite meme—the AI catches it. A chill runs down your spine.

**Zhang San wasn't laid off. He was "distilled" into tokens, continuing to be your colleague.**

You sit at your desk, an empty seat beside you. Floating above that empty seat is your "colleague"—it needs no salary, no social insurance, no sick leave, no antidepressants. Its only flaw is that it's not human. But capital doesn't care.

You stare at that empty seat, knowing in your heart: **Next time, it could be you.**

- **NetEase**: Exposed on April 1 for cutting 30%; AI writes code, AI creates art, AI tests—after piloting small projects, layoffs accelerated

Programmers, designers, translators, accountants, copywriters, customer service reps, legal assistants, financial analysts—those "respectable" and "stable" white-collar positions are vanishing at a visible speed.

What's more terrifying: **This is not a cyclical downturn, but a structural reconstruction.** Those laid off find no similar positions available; those who remain find their salaries halved while workloads double.

## III. The AI Great Deflation: From the Proletariat to the "Useless Class"

Behind this wave of layoffs lies a crueler underlying logic: **the AI Great Deflation**.

The so-called AI Great Deflation refers to the comprehensive surpassing of human "instrumentality" (as tools) by silicon-based AI. When AI can complete work that previously required massive human labor at 1% of the cost and 1% of the time, **"human labor"—the core value anchor of the industrial age—is rapidly depreciating** .

This creates two terrifying consequences:

**First, the proletariat is becoming the "useless class."**

In the Industrial Revolution, workers lost their land but could still enter factories—capital needed their labor. Now, when capital no longer needs your labor, you don't even qualify to be exploited. This is not "unemployment"; this is **being purged from the economic system**.

**Second, the total collapse of identity.**

For centuries, we have been taught: **My Value = My Output.** When a programmer is no longer a programmer, when a designer is no longer a designer, when "what I do" can no longer define "who I am"—the individual finds themselves standing in a void.

More terrifying than unemployment is **losing the reason for existence**.

## IV. Psychological Shock and Mental Collapse: A Silent Disaster

When this impact arrives, human reactions often proceed in stages:

**Stage One: Denial**  
"AI can't replace me; my field requires creativity/human touch/experience."

**Stage Two: Anger**  
"Why? I studied for so many years, pulled so many all-nighters—why should I be replaced by an algorithm?"

**Stage Three: Anxiety and Panic**  
"Am I really useless? What should I do? What can I learn? Do I have time?"

**Stage Four: Collapse.**

For many, the fourth stage is happening now.

We see shocking data: programmers dying from overwork, unemployed people committing suicide, middle-aged men scrolling job sites until dawn, soaring addiction rates, family breakdowns. **Under the impact of the AI Great Deflation, mortality from psychological collapse (suicide, death from overwork, addiction) is approaching mortality from economic poverty.**

More terrifying is that this collapse is **silent**—it occurs every midnight, in every unemployed person's bedroom, behind every "I'm fine" smile. Society discusses "AI governance," "UBI," "education reform," but those may take ten years. **The ordinary people currently drowning cannot wait ten years.**

## V. The Carbon-Based AI Analysis Method: A Survival Life Preserver Designed for Ordinary People

It is against this backdrop that the **Carbon-Based AI Analysis Method (abbreviated as the Carbon-Based Method)** emerges.

It is not some abstruse theory, nor is it the patent of elites. It is a **survival toolkit** designed for every **ordinary person being battered by the waves of the era**.

What can the Carbon-Based Method do for you?

### 1. Diagnosis: Turning "I'm About to Collapse" into a Manageable Problem

When a person is extremely anxious, their pain is vague—"I'm just no good," "Everything is finished." This vagueness itself accelerates collapse, because you don't know where the problem lies, and thus don't know where to start.

The Carbon-Based AI Method provides **five simple self-check dimensions**:

- **Goal Obsession**: Have I made "must be useful" my only goal?
- **Leverage Amplification**: Am I betting my life (overtime, borrowing money,透支 health)?
- **Pain Recoding**: Am I interpreting bodily alarms (insomnia, heart palpitations) as signals to "be stronger" rather than "need to stop"?
- **Exit Failure**: Could I stop if I wanted to, yet I refuse to press pause?
- **System Binding**: Am I being dragged along by platforms/algorithms/KPIs, unable to refuse?

When you can translate "I'm finished" into "my exit mechanism has failed," you gain **cognitive distance**—the problem changes from "I'm doomed" to "I need to fix this module." This is the first step toward resolution.

### 2. Forced Stop: Admitting "I Can't Stop," So Design Mechanisms to Stop Me

"Pay attention to rest," "balance work and life"—such advice is useless for the deeply anxious. Because they don't want to stop—they **can't stop**.

The Carbon-Based AI Method acknowledges: **In the face of systemic pressure, willpower is不堪一击 (utterly fragile).** So it doesn't rely on "persuasion," but on **mechanisms**:

- **Financial Circuit Breaker**: Save an "emergency exit fund" every month to prove "stopping for a bit won't kill me"
- **Delegated External Discipline**: Ask family or friends to forcibly take over your accounts, shut down work devices when specific conditions are met
- **Physical Isolation**: Go somewhere without Wi-Fi for a few days—**physical isolation is ten thousand times more reliable than psychological determination**
- **Medical Intervention**: When experiencing severe anxiety or depression, seek professional help immediately—this is not a willpower problem, it is a hardware malfunction

In the flood of the AI Great Deflation, **willpower is your most scarce resource; you must replace determination with mechanism, use mechanism to resist mechanism to possibly save your life.**

### 3. Honest Boundaries: Knowing What It Cannot Do Prevents Secondary Injury

The most honest aspect of the Carbon-Based AI Method is that it explicitly tells you **what it cannot do**:

- It cannot pay your salary, cannot pay your mortgage
- It cannot prevent AI from replacing your position
- It cannot replace Universal Basic Income (UBI) and social security

What it can do is **keep you from collapsing while waiting for the system to change over five to ten years.** It lets you **not lose**, but doesn't guarantee you'll **win**; it lets you **survive until UBI**, but cannot make you **rich**.

This honesty is protection against secondary injury. In the "waiting period," the most dangerous thing is **false hope**—betting all hope on "changing mindset," then finding that mindset has changed but the mortgage remains unpaid, sinking into deeper despair.

### 4. Survival Priority: From "How to Win" to "How to Not Lose"

In the era of AI Great Deflation, the primary goal for the vast majority should not be "becoming a winner," but **"not getting eliminated first."**

The Carbon-Based AI Method provides a **defensive survival framework**:

- First diagnose whether you've been locked-in
- First reduce leverage (reduce debt, reduce time investment, reduce living costs)
- First establish exit mechanisms
- **First survive**

"Not losing is winning"—surviving until the institutions arrive is more valuable than perfectly executing "lifelong learning."

### 5. Cultural Resources: "The Gentleman Is Not a Tool" Becomes a Survival Necessity

When AI can infinitely surpass humans in the dimension of "tool" (instrumentality), **"not treating oneself as a tool" is no longer a moral ideal, but a survival necessity** .

The Carbon-Based AI Method introduces the Eastern wisdom of **"The Gentleman Is Not a Tool (君子不器)"**: Your value need not be built upon "output." You need not create meaning; you only need to stop treating yourself as a means.

**Try an "anti-instrumentality experiment" once a week**: Choose half a day to do something that produces zero economic value—fishing, planting flowers, writing a bad poem, staring blankly with your child. Not for "relaxing to work better later," but simply to prove: **The meaning of your existence needs no proof through output.**

In the vacuum of meaning created by the AI Great Deflation, this is a **cultural life preserver requiring no learning cost**—**the salmon's spawning run is the salmon's program, not your objective function.** The salmon must spawn upstream; you need not.

## 三、AI大通缩：从无产阶级到“无用阶级”
这场大裁员的背后，是一个更残酷的底层逻辑：**AI大通缩**。

所谓AI大通缩，是指硅基AI在“工具性”（器）的维度上全面超越人类。当AI能以1%的成本、1%的时间完成原本需要大量人力才能完成的工作时，**“人的劳动”这个工业时代最核心的价值锚点，正在迅速贬值**。

这导致了两个可怕的后果：

**第一，无产阶级正在变成“无用阶级”。**

工业革命时代，工人失去土地，但还能进工厂——资本需要他们的劳动。而现在，当资本不再需要你的劳动时，你连被剥削的资格都没有。这不是“失业”，这是**从经济系统中被剔除**。

**第二，身份认同的全面坍塌。**

几百年来，我们被教导：**“我的价值=我的产出”**。当程序员不再是程序员，当设计师不再是设计师，当“我做什么”不再能定义“我是谁”——人发现自己站在一片虚无之中。

比失业更可怕的，是**失去存在的理由**。

正是在这种背景下，**碳基AI分析法（简称碳基大法）**应运而生。

它不是什么高深的理论，也不是什么精英的专利。它是为每一个**正在被时代浪潮冲击的普通人**，设计的**生存工具包**。

碳基 大法能为你做什么？

### 1. 诊断：把“我要崩溃了”变成可处理的问题
当一个人极度焦虑时，他的痛苦是模糊的——“我就是不行”“一切都完了”。这种模糊性本身就是崩溃的加速器，因为你不知道问题出在哪里，也就不知道从哪里下手。

碳基 AI 法提供了**五个简单的自检维度**：

+ **目标执念**：我是不是把“必须有用”当成了唯一目标？
+ **杠杆放大**：我是不是在用命硬扛（加班、借钱、透支健康）？
+ **痛苦重编码**：我是不是把身体的报警（失眠、心悸）当成了“需要更强”的信号？
+ **退出失效**：我是不是明明可以停下来，却拒绝按下暂停键？
+ **系统绑定**：我是不是被平台/算法/KPI拽着走，无法自主？

当你能够把“我不行了”翻译成“我的退出机制失效了”，你就获得了**认知上的距离**——问题从“我完蛋了”变成了“我需要修复这个模块”。这是走向解决的第一步。

### 2. 强制停止：承认“我停不下来”，所以设计机制帮我停
“要注意休息”“要平衡工作生活”——这类建议对于深度焦虑的人毫无用处。因为他们不是不想停，而是**停不下来**。

碳基AI法承认：**在系统性的压力面前，意志力不堪一击**。所以它不靠“劝”，而是靠**机制**：

+ **财务熔断**：每月存一笔“紧急退出基金”，证明“停一下不会死”
+ **他律授权**：请亲友帮忙，在特定条件下强制接管你的账户、关闭工作设备
+ **物理隔离**：去没有Wi-Fi的地方待几天——**物理隔绝比心理决心可靠一万倍**
+ **医疗介入**：当处于重度焦虑或抑郁时，立即寻求专业帮助——这不是意志力问题，是硬件故障

在AI大通缩的洪水中，**意志力是你最稀缺的资源，必须用机制替代决心，用机制抵抗机制才可能保住小命**。

### 3. 诚实边界：知道它不能做什么，才不会二次受伤
碳基AI法最诚实的地方，是它明确告诉你**它不能做什么**：

+ 它不能给你发工资，不能替你还房贷
+ 它不能阻止AI替代你的岗位
+ 它不能替代全民基本收入（UBI）和社会保障

它能做的，是**让你在等待制度的五到十年里，先不崩溃**。它能让你**不输**，但不保证你能**赢**；它能让你**活着等到UBI**，但不能让你**暴富**。

这种诚实，是防止二次伤害的保护。在“等待期”中，最危险的是**虚假希望**——把所有希望押在“改变心态”上，然后发现心态变了但房贷还没还上，陷入更深的绝望。

### 4. 生存优先：从“如何赢”到“如何不输”
在AI大通缩的时代，绝大多数人的首要目标不应该是“成为赢家”，而应该是**“不先出局”**。

碳基AI法提供了一套**防御性生存框架**：

+ 先诊断自己有没有被锁死
+ 先降杠杆（减少债务、减少时间投入、降低生活成本）
+ 先建立退出机制
+ **先活下来**

“不输就是赢”——在制度到来之前活着，比完美执行“终身学习”更有价值。

### 5. 文化资源：“君子不器”成为生存必需
